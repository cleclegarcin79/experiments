<sent id="11">  Corelli was a man of gentle disposition and simple habits . </sent> <sent id="12">  His plainness of dress and freedom from ostentation gave the impression that he was parsimonious , and Handel says of him that `` he liked nothing better than seeing pictures without paying for it , and saving money , '' He was also noted for his objection to riding in carriages . </sent> <sent id="13">  He lived on terms of intimacy with the leading artists of his time , and had a great fondness for pictures , of which he had a valuable collection . </sent>
