<sent id="43">  The medial temporal and parietal areas of the brain are typically the first areas to appear shrunken as neurons die . </sent> <sent id="44">  Shrinkage is particularly early and prominent in the hippocampi , structures that are needed for making new memories , and in the posterior parietal lobes , which are involved in spatial skills , navigation , and calculation . </sent> <sent id="45">  Later , nearly the entire brain appears shrunken , and the spaces within the brain , called ventricles , grow larger . </sent>
